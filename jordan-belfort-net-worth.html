Jordan Belfort Net Worth: How the Wolf of Wall Street Made and Lost His Fortune

Jordan Belfort is a former stockbroker, author, and motivational speaker most known for his role in one of history’s largest financial scams.

His extravagant and reckless lifestyle was dramatized in the 2013 film The Wolf of Wall Street, starring Leonardo DiCaprio. But how much is Jordan Belfort worth today, and how did he come by his fortune? This article will look at his career, accomplishments, and net worth.

Early Life and Career Beginnings

Jordan Belfort was born on July 9, 1962, to a Jewish household in Queens, New York. He grew raised in a middle-class area, the son of accountants. He studied biology at American University and graduated with honors.

He then enrolled in dental school but dropped out after the dean informed him that dentistry was not a lucrative profession. Belfort began his career as a door-to-door salesman of meat and fish. He went on to become a stockbroker, and in 1987, he co-founded Stratton Oakmont with his friend Danny Porush.

The firm specializes in penny stocks, which are low-cost, high-risk shares of small businesses. Belfort and his brokers employed aggressive and dishonest tactics to convince investors to acquire these stocks, increasing their prices and reaping massive commissions.

Rise to Fame and Success

Using a “pump and dump” strategy, Belfort and his firm made millions of dollars in the late 1980s and early 1990s. They would artificially inflate the values of the stocks they owned before selling them at a large profit before the prices fell.

They also engaged in illicit operations such as money laundering, tax evasion, and insider trading. Belfort earned the moniker “Wolf of Wall Street” and led a lavish lifestyle. He had a Long Island house, a yacht, a helicopter, and several luxury cars.

He also spent a fortune on drugs, drink, prostitutes, parties, and travel. He was married twice, and his second wife, Nadine Caridi, had two children.

Belfort’s peak net worth was believed to be over $200 million, although some sources believe it was more than $1 billion. He was one of Wall Street’s most successful and infamous stockbrokers, with a cult-like following among his workers and clientele.

Fall from Grace and Legal Troubles

Belfort’s fraudulent and unlawful activities eventually drew the notice of authorities, who investigated him with the FBI, the SEC, and the NASD. He was arrested and accused of securities fraud and money laundering in 1998.

In exchange for a lower sentence, he pled guilty and agreed to collaborate with the government. He also agreed to repay $110 million to the investors he duped. Belfort was sentenced to four years in prison in 2003, but he only served 22 months of that time in a minimum-security facility.

He was also forced to give up the majority of his possessions, including his mansion, yacht, and cars. He was released in 2006 and sentenced to pay 50% of his earnings to the reparations fund until his debt was paid off.

Jordan Belfort Career and Current Net Worth

Belfort reinvented himself as an author and motivational speaker after his release from prison. He documented his rise and fall as a stockbroker in two memoirs, The Wolf of Wall Street (2007) and Catching the Wolf of Wall Street (2009).

Martin Scorsese adapted the books into a film starring Leonardo DiCaprio as Belfort, which was a critical and commercial success. Belfort has founded his own company, Belfort Training, which provides business and individual sales training and coaching.

Conclusion

Jordan Belfort, a contentious and interesting figure, has seen riches and renown rise and fall. A powerful and rich stockbroker, he made and lost millions through deception and manipulation. After being convicted, he went to prison and lost most of his possessions.

He is now a prominent author and speaker who teaches sales and persuasion. He is notorious, owes his victims a lot, and has legal and ethical issues. He is a Wall Street wolf who shaped finance and culture.
