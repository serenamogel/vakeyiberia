Is 28 Days Haunted Scripted?

28 Days Haunted is a real paranormal experimental show. It showcases the reason behind the haunted house and tries to solve the problem.

The show is based on the theory of Ed and Lorraine, who passed away before proving it. They had learned that it took 28 days to know about living and gone souls.

The New England Society of Psychic Research (NESPR), a branch of the Warrens Paranormal Research Society, is responsible for carrying out the study.

Tony Spera, Warren's son-in-law is the host of Netflix's 28 Days Haunted. He, along with Paranormal Journalist, Aaron Sagers, was seen in the control room. 

They are the ones who observe and control the activity performed in three different locations and manage the footage from all the locations.

None of the team members knew where they would stay for their month-long experiment.

They were all blindfolded and were taken to the location where there was no access to the outer world.

Among the three-team, the first group traveled to the Lumber Baron Inn in Denver, Colorado, which includes paranormal investigators Amy Parks, Ray Cozzi, and Shane Pittman.

Another group went to Madison Dry Goods in Madison, North Carolina. This team only had two members, Brandy Marie Miller ad Jereme Leonard who is a demonologist.

The last group, consisting of psychic medium Sean Austin, paranormal investigators, and tech experts Nick Simons and Aaron Thompson, traveled to Captain Grant's Inn in Preston, Connecticut.

The program provides information on various mediums for interacting with ghosts, including radios, mirrors, candles, and more. All of these methods give insights into the psychic in communication with spirits.

During the month-long filming of the experimental series, more than 2000 hours of footage was filmed between the three teams.
