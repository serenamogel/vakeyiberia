Carl Icahn Net Worth and Income

As of December 2023, the successful investor Carl Icahn’s net worth is $ 16.8 billion.

Icahn started his business with $150,000 of his own money and a $400,000 investment from his uncle, M. Elliot Schnall, and founded Icahn & Co., specializing in risk arbitrage and options trading.

In the 1980s, he doubled his money on Texaco, made $800 million on Nabisco, and made nearly $3 billion on CVR Energy.

Similarly, he won the first proxy fight over stover stone maker Tappan for a profit of 193% in 1979.

The majority of Carl Icahn’s net worth comes from his holdings in “Icahn Enterprises,” a diversified holding company, as well as his successful investments in various enterprises and ventures.

In 2013, Carl bought almost $2 billion in Dell stock when the company planned to go private.

In 2017, Icahn sold X.O. Holdings to Verizon for $1.8 billion. Icahn sold the unfinished Fontainebleau Resort Las Vegas for $600 million in August 2017, more than four times the price he paid.

His fortune is currently split between publicly traded Icahn Enterprises, his family company Icahn Partners, and a diverse portfolio of personal investments in real estate and railcars.

According to Forbes, the daring businessman was named one of the top five highest-earning hedge fund managers in the world in 2015.

Carl Icahn | Lifestyle

Lifestyle

Icahn grew up in Queens, the son of a synagogue cantor and a high school teacher, and went to public school before enrolling at Princeton University to study philosophy.

He developed his interest in investing after experiences in medical school and the service.

Charity

Carl signed the pledge 20 years ago, much like other billionaires who pledge to donate their wealth to charity. He has promised to donate $20 billion to charity.

His charity, the Children’s Rescue Fund, has established a 65-unit building for homeless families of unmarried pregnant women and single mothers with children.

He also opened a homeless shelter in New York City, naming them Icahn House East and Icahn House West.

Similarly, The Carl C. Icahn Center for Science and Icahn Scholar Program at Choate Rosemary Hall provides for ten students’ tuition, lodging and board, books, and supplies for four years (freshman–senior), with an endowment of around $400,000 per year.

Carl Icahn | Investment & Books

Investment

Icahn is an activist investor who buys shares in companies, sometimes in tiny amounts, giving him power and a stick to use in shareholder votes to force changes on the board of directors.

According to annual SEC filings, the billionaire invests his wealth through multiple businesses, including Icahn Partners, in which Icahn Enterprises is the only other shareholder.

The value of Icahn Partners’ holdings is published around once a quarter in Icahn Enterprises regulatory filings.

He also owns Icahn Enterprises Holdings, the parent firm of publicly-traded Icahn Enterprises, with a stake of 1.99 percent.

Carl got himself into trouble in 2014, as the SEC looked into his financial decisions. His equities had lost about 4% of their value at the time, and he had lost $420 million in a single day.

Despite this, Carl Icahn still holds the title of richest trader on Wall Street, with a net worth of $ 23.6 billion.

Icahn made a $100 million investment in Lyft on May 15, 2015.

In November 2015, he also exchanged his eBay stock for PayPal stock. Icahn also declared a 7.13 percent investment in Xerox in November 2015.

Carl Icahn | Career

Initially, Icahn was a registered sales representative on Wall Street in 1961, and by the end of the decade, he built up a large enough client base to open his brokerage firm.

He started brokerage Icahn & Co. by borrowing $400,000, and he has built up a billion-dollar fortune for himself.

Carl’s one of the renowned milestones is, buying TWA outright. This sale netted him $469 million and left TWA with a $540 million debt. Then, in 1991, he sold TWA’s coveted London routes for $445 million to American airlines.

In recent years, he’s also gotten media attention for endorsing Donald Trump’s candidacy, arguing that the then-nominee was a “positive” for the U.S. economy, not a “negative.”

Carl Icahn is also featured in the HBO documentary “Icahn: The Restless Billionaire”, which was released in February 2022.
