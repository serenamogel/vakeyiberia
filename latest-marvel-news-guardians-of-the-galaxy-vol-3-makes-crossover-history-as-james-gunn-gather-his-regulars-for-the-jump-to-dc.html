Latest Marvel News: Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 makes crossover history as James Gunn gathe

Now that Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 is out there for the world to see, fans can comb through every frame on repeat viewings to try and dig up new treats, even if the first-ever crossover between the Marvel Cinematic Universe and DCU may have gone completely unnoticed.

Meanwhile, James Gunn has confirmed what everybody knew was going to happen anyway, all while taking the time to debunk a long-held theory about Captain America: The First Avenger that plenty of people would love to be true. There’s never a dull moment for the world’s biggest franchise, and that isn’t going to change anytime soon.

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 sees Marvel cross-pollinate with DC at long last

Everyone thought James Gunn was only talking a good game when he claimed a crossover between Marvel and DC’s cinematic universes was potentially more than a pipe dream, something he sneakily proved in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3.

The writer and director confirmed that Peacemaker scene-stealer Eagly makes a cameo appearance as a member of the High Evolutionary’s menagerie, making the majestic bird the first DCU favorite to show up in the rival superhero sandbox.

In breaking news, James Gunn will cast his favorites in Superman: Legacy

We all know Superman: Legacy is going to feature at least one or two of James Gunn’s regular cohorts, but he’s nonetheless narrowed the field after confirming that at least one member of his Guardians of the Galaxy ensemble is going to swing by.

That hardly whittles down the candidates exponentially, but there’s going to be backlash either way when the DCU reboot’s roster is announced, with a smattering of the co-CEO’s repertory destined for either supporting or major parts.

For the last time, Captain America is not Peter Quill’s grandfather

One of the most outlandish theories to ensnare the MCU fandom has been percolating in the background for over a decade, with Laura Haddock playing both a Captain America fangirl in The First Avenger and Meredith Quill in Guardians of the Galaxy.

Understandably, many joined the dots and reached the conclusion that Steve Rogers was actually Peter Quill’s grandfather, and even though James Gunn has denied at least once before, he felt compelled to do so again so nobody messes up his Guardians canon.

That’s it for another week of Marvel news, but as always, the cycle begins anew tomorrow.
